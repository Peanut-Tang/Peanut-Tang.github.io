About me

I am a senior student majoring in computer science and technology in the School of the Gifted Young, University of Science and Technology of China (USTC). I'm fortunate to be advised by Prof. Xue Chen.

Overall, I have a broad interest in theoretical computer science, particularly in areas related to pseudorandomness, computational complexity and cryptography. I have conducted research on the design of PRGs, hash functions and error-correcting codes. At the same time, I am passionate about exploring combinatorics and probability in pure mathematics, as well as optimization problems in computational or financial mathematics.

Starting in September 2026, I will be pursuing my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the Center on Frontiers of Computing Studies (CFCS), Peking University (PKU), under the supervision of Prof. Kuan Cheng and Prof. Tianren Liu.

In Fall 2025, I visited Prof. Xin Li's group at Johns Hopkins University (JHU) as a research intern. And currently, I also continue to work with Prof. Xin Li remotely.
